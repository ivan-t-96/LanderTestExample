What Is the Timeshare Cancellation Roster?

The 2026 Timeshare Cancellation Roster is a regularly updated reference compiled by consumer contract specialists that identifies resort properties where documented patterns of problematic sales practices have been established. Properties are categorized by the strength of the legal grounds available to owners seeking cancellation.

The Roster Has Three Categories:
  • Immediate Cancellation — Resort has documented findings of misrepresentation or illegal sales tactics. Strongest grounds, fastest process, refund potential.
  • Priority Review — Resort shows consistent pattern of undisclosed fee escalations or verbal promises not reflected in contracts. Strong cancellation grounds.
  • Standard Review — Resort does not appear on prior watchlists but individual contract circumstances may still qualify for cancellation.

Owners whose resorts appear under Immediate Cancellation or Priority Review typically have the strongest cases — and the fastest timelines. Sandra's case resolved in under five months from first contact to signed cancellation letter.

Why So Many Owners Don't Know They Qualify

The timeshare industry has long relied on one core assumption: that owners believe their contracts are airtight. Sales presentations routinely emphasized permanence — "this is a lifetime investment," "your family can enjoy this for generations" — while glossing over the legal realities of how those contracts were sold.

Consumer contract specialists say that's precisely where the vulnerability lies. It's not about breaking a contract. It's about examining how it was sold — and whether the sales process itself created grounds for cancellation.

Common Grounds Found in Roster Properties:
  • Verbal promises during sales presentations that contradict the written contract
  • Maintenance fee escalations that were never disclosed at time of purchase
  • Misrepresentation of resale value or exit options
  • High-pressure sales environments that may violate consumer protection statutes
  • Contracts signed without adequate review time or rescission period disclosure

For owners at properties on the Roster, this review process is significantly shorter because the groundwork has largely already been established. Instead of building a case from scratch, specialists can apply findings already documented against the resort to each individual owner's situation.

That's what made Sandra's case move as quickly as it did. Her resort had been on the Immediate Cancellation list since late 2025. The moment her contract was reviewed against existing findings, her path forward was clear.

"Nobody told me this was possible. I spent three years paying for something I was told I'd never escape. The Roster changed everything."

What Happens After You Check the Roster

For owners whose resorts appear on the list, the next step is a case evaluation — a detailed review of the original purchase circumstances, contract terms, and payment history. This is where specialists determine which category of grounds applies and what the realistic timeline and outcome looks like.

Not every case results in a refund. Refund eligibility depends on the specific findings attached to your resort's listing, how long you've been paying, and what documentation is available from the original sale. But for Immediate Cancellation properties specifically, refund review is a standard part of the process.

For every owner, the outcome they care most about — getting out of the contract itself — is the primary goal. And for those whose resorts appear on the 2026 Roster, that outcome is more achievable right now than it has ever been.
